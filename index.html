Stress and Vowel Length

Latin had no stress accents like English; instead, stress fell by rule: in words of two syllables, stress is always on the first. In longer words, stress falls on the penultimate syllable if it is long, otherwise on the antepenultimate. Classical Latin also distinguished long and short vowels, written in modern editions with macrons: mƒÅlum (apple) vs malum (evil).
